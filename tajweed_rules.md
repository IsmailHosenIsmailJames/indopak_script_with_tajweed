# Tajweed Rules Guide

This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the Tajweed rules used in the recitation of the Holy Quran. Mastering these rules helps in reciting the Quran with correct pronunciation and rhythm, as revealed to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).

---

## 1. Ghunnah (Nasalization) | الغنة
**Definition:** A nasal sound produced from the nose, held for approximately 2 counts.
**Letters:** It applies to the letters **Noon (ن)** and **Meem (م)** when they have a Shaddah (w sign).
**How to Pronounce:** Press onto the letter and hold the nasal sound.

* **Example:** (إِنَّ) `Inna` - (ثُمَّ) `Thumma`

---

## 2. Nun Sakinah & Tanween Rules | أحكام النون الساكنة والتنوين
These rules apply when a **Nun Sakinah (نْ)** (Nun with no vowel or with a Sukoon) or **Tanween** (double vowels: ً ٍ ٌ) is followed by specific letters.

### A. Idgham (Merging) | الإدغام
Merging the Nun/Tanween into the next letter. There are two types:

#### i. Idgham with Ghunnah (Merging with Nasalization)
**Letters:** (ي ن م و) - Mnemonic: **"Yanmu"**
**Rule:** If Nun/Tanween is followed by any of these letters, merge the Nun sound into the next letter and hold the Ghunnah for 2 counts.
**Example:** (مَن يَقُولُ) `May-yaqool`

#### ii. Idgham without Ghunnah (Merging without Nasalization)
**Letters:** (ل ر)
**Rule:** If Nun/Tanween is followed by Lam or Ra, merge completely without any nasal sound. The Nun disappears entirely.
**Example:** (مِن لَدُنْهُ) `Mil-ladunhu` - (غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ) `Ghafoorur-Raheem`

### B. Iqlab (Transformation) | الإقلاب
**Letter:** (ب)
**Rule:** If Nun/Tanween is followed by Ba, the Nun sound turns into a hidden **Meem (م)** with a Ghunnah.
**Example:** (مِن بَعْدِ) `Mim-ba'd`

### C. Ikhfa (Hiding) | الإخفاء
**Letters:** Any letter **except** the ones for Izhar (Throat letters), Idgham, or Iqlab. (15 letters: ت ث ج د ذ ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ف ق ك)
**Rule:** Hide the Nun sound, making a sound between Izhar and Idgham, with a Ghunnah. The sound's thickness depends on the following letter.
**Example:** (الإِنسَانُ) `Al-Insaan`

---

## 3. Meem Sakinah Rules | أحكام الميم الساكنة
These rules apply to **Meem Sakinah (مْ)**.

### A. Idgham Shafawi (Lip-to-Lip Merging)
**Letter:** (م)
**Rule:** When Meem Sakinah is followed by another Meem, merge them with a Ghunnah.
**Example:** (لَكُم مَّا) `Lakum-ma`

### B. Ikhfa Shafawi (Lip-to-Lip Hiding)
**Letter:** (ب)
**Rule:** When Meem Sakinah is followed by Ba, conceal the Meem slightly with a Ghunnah, keeping the lips in a light contact or leaving a tiny gap.
**Example:** (تَرْمِيهِم بِحِجَارَةٍ) `Tarmeehim-bi-hijarah`

---

## 4. Qalqalah (Echoing) | القلقلة
**Letters:** (ق ط ب ج د) - Mnemonic: **"Qutb Jad"**
**Rule:** When these letters have a Sukoon (either usually or due to stopping), they are pronounced with a strong "bounce" or echo sound.
**Levels:**
1. **strongest:** At the end of a word with Shaddah (e.g., الْحَقُّ).
2. **Strong:** At the end of a word (e.g., الْفَلَقِ).
3. **Light:** In the middle of a word (e.g., يَدْعُونَ).

---

## 5. Madd (Prolongation) | المد
Madd is the stretching of a sound. The basic Madd letters are:
- **Alif (ا)** preceded by Fatha.
- **Waw (و)** preceded by Dammah.
- **Ya (ي)** preceded by Kasrah.

### A. Madd Tabi'i (Natural Madd)
**Rule:** Stretched for **2 counts**. It is the natural length of the vowel.
**Example:** (قَالَ) `Qaala`

### B. Madd Wajib Muttasil (Obligatory Connected Madd)
**Rule:** A Madd letter is followed by a **Hamzah (ء)** in the **same word**.
**Duration:** 4 or 5 counts.
**Example:** (السَّمَاءِ) `As-Samaa'`

### C. Madd Ja'iz Munfasil (Permissible Separate Madd)
**Rule:** A Madd letter is at the end of a word, and **Hamzah (ء)** is at the start of the next word.
**Duration:** 4 or 5 counts (can be 2 in some recitation modes).
**Example:** (يَا أَيُّهَا) `Yaa-Ayyuha`

### D. Madd Lazim (Compulsory Madd)
**Rule:** A Madd letter is followed by a **permanent Sukoon** or **Shaddah**.
**Duration:** 6 counts (full length).
**Example:** (الضَّالِّينَ) `Ad-Daalleen`

### E. Madd Leen (Soft Madd)
**Letters:** Waw (و) or Ya (ي) with Sukoon, preceded by Fatha.
**Rule:** Occurs only when stopping on the word. Stretched for 2, 4, or 6 counts.
**Example:** (خَوْفٌ) `Khawf` - (الْبَيْتِ) `Bayt`

## 6. Miscellaneous Rules

### Hamzat al-Wasl (Connecting Hamzah)
**Symbol:** (ٱ) or implied at the start of words like (ال).
**Rule:** Pronounced when starting the speech, but dropped (silent) when continuing from a previous word.
**Example:** (ٱهْدِنَا) - Pronounced "Ihdina" if starting, but silent in "Wa-hdina".

### Lam Shamsiyyah (Sun Lam)
**Rule:** The Lam (ل) of "Al" (ال) represents a "Sun Letter". The Lam is written but **not pronounced**. The next letter gets a Shaddah.
**Letters:** (ت ث د ذ ر ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ل ن)
**Example:** (الشَّمْسُ) `Ash-Shamsu`

### Silent Letters
**Rule:** Letters that appear in the script but are not pronounced.
**Example:** The Alif in (قَالُوا۟) - The final Alif is silent.

### Idgham Mutajanisayn (Homogeneous)
**Rule:** Merging two letters that share the **same articulation point** (Makhraj) but differ in characteristics. Look for a Sukoon on the first and Shaddah on the second.
**Common Pairs:** (ت into ط), (ط into ت), (ت into د), (د into ت), (ذ into ظ), (ب into م).
**Example:** (قَد تَبَيَّنَ) `Qat-tabayyana`

### Idgham Mutaqaribayn (Close/Similar)
**Rule:** Merging letters that are **close** in articulation point and characteristics.
**Common Pairs:** (ل into ر), (ق into ك).
**Example:** (أَلَمْ نَخْلُقكُّم) `Nakhlukkum`

### Alif Maqsurah
**Rule:** A dotless Ya (ى) containing a short vertical Alif sound. Pronounced as a normal Alif (Madd Tabi'i).
**Example:** (مُوسَىٰ) `Musa`
